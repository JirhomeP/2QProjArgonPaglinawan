Backstroke

The backstroke, or back crawl, uses alternating and opposite arm movements.

As one arm pulls through the water from an overhead position to the hip, the other arm recovers above the water from the hip to the overhead position and vice versa.

The backstroke is, as the name suggests, the only one of the four competitive swimming strokes swum on the back.

In terms of speed, it is slower than the front crawl and butterfly but faster than the breaststroke.
